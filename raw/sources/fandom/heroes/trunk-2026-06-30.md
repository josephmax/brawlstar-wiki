Trunk is an {{Rarity|Epic}} [[Brawler]] who has high health, a variable damage output and movement speed, a short range, and immense utility with their main attack and Super. They were the 94th Brawler added to the game. Trunk's Trait allows them to charge their Super from enemies damaging them. They attack by spinning around after a short delay, which instantly deals damage in a circular area around them and leaves behind ants that give them a movement speed and a damage buff. For their Super, Trunk dashes forward, leaving behind ants on the ground that behave identically to their main attack. Their first Gadget, For the Queen!, instantly drops a large area of ants. Their second Gadget, Worker Ants, heals Trunk after being hit by a single projectile. Their first Star Power, New Insect Overlords, moderately decreases the damage output of enemies standing on ants. Their second Star Power, Colony Scouts, reveals enemies in bushes that are standing on ants. Their Hypercharge, Retread, makes their ants from their Super deal damage overtime.

### Trait

{{Quote|This Brawler charges Super from damage received.}}{{Trait|Tank}}Whenever Trunk takes damage from enemy Brawlers or spawnables, they charge their Super. The Super charge rate depends on the damage the enemy deals. It takes 2.4x Trunk's max health of damage to charge their Super completely, and all damage is calculated after shielding and other effects.

### Attack: Ant Attack!

{{Quote|Trunk spins in a circle after a short delay, dealing damage to enemies and dropping ants. Standing near ants gives Trunk a 25% movement speed boost and 30% damage boost. The edge of the attack deals more damage to enemies.}}
After a 0.35-second delay, Trunk damages all enemies in a radius around them, including enemies behind walls, similar to [[Jacky]] and [[Doug]]. However, if Trunk damages enemies at the edge of the radius, the attack deals more damage. More specifically, the centre of the enemy's hitbox must be within 0.67 tiles of the edge. The damage is dealt immediately without any travel time, but Trunk has a mediocre speed and attack cooldown. There is no direction of travel to this attack, so aiming is not required, though aiming can be used to show you the area-of-effect. In addition, 0.3 seconds after attacking, Trunk drops an area of ants, where if they stand on them, they gain a 25% movement speed buff and a 30% damage boost. The area of ants has a diameter of 3.33 tiles and lasts for 20 seconds. Trunk's attack can be cancelled if they are stunned, knocked back, or pulled during the delay; however, their ammo will be retained.

### Super: Driven by Revenge (and Ants)

{{Quote|Trunk propels themselves at enemies, dropping ants along the way. They can bounce off walls too!}}
Trunk dashes forward, dropping ants following the Super's path, and gaining a 50% damage reduction shield for 0.9 seconds. These ants behave identically to those of their main attack. While Trunk is using their Super on a target, they will move slower, dealing low damage continuously every 0.05 seconds. Trunk can use their Super to dash over water, and can bounce off walls, but if hit by a stun or pull while their Super is active, it is cancelled. Rolling over water can extend their Super range to a certain amount. Unlike most other abilities, Trunk cannot cancel or interrupt their main attack with their Super.

### Gadgets / For the Queen!

{{Quote|Trunk instantly spreads ants in a large area. Cooldown: 7 seconds.}}{{Gadget|1}}
Activating this Gadget allows Trunk to instantly spawn a circular area of ants that has a 70% larger radius, making it much larger than that of their main attack. The ants spawned with this Gadget behave identically to that of their main attack, outside of being unaffected by their Star Powers.

### Gadgets / Worker Ants

{{Quote|Trunk heals from the first source of damage taken for the next 2 secs. Cooldown: 16 seconds.}}{{Gadget|2}}
When activated, Trunk will gain a special shield that lasts for 2 seconds. The next time they take damage, instead of losing health, they instead gain how much health the attack would have taken away. Unlike [[Nani]]'s Return to Sender Gadget and [[Surge]]'s Power Shield Gadget, and like [[Kenji]]'s Nigiri Nemesis Star Power, this effect can apply to more than one projectile as long as the damage is done in the same frame (for example, multiple projectiles from [[Shelly]]'s Super). This Gadget's cooldown starts after it is used, not after the shield duration ends.

### Star Powers / New Insect Overlords

{{Quote|Ants also reduce the damage of enemies by 15%.}}{{StarPower|1}}
Trunk's ants apply an attack debuff on enemies, decreasing the damage output of enemies affected by 15%. This Star Power only affects damage abilities and only applies to ants from their main attack. Affected enemies have an additional visual effect to indicate that this effect is active; there will be red arrows pointing down on the enemy. The effect is lost when the enemy leaves the ants.

### Star Powers / Colony Scouts

{{StarPower|2}}
{{Quote|Ants reveal hidden enemies.}}Trunk's ants reveal enemies in bushes and invisible enemies.

### Hypercharge: Retread

{{Quote|Trunk's Super causes them to leave ants on enemies which deal damage over time.}}{{Hypercharge}}
When activated, Trunk's Super no longer makes them move slower upon hitting a target. Trunk's dash only deals initial impact damage, but leaves ants on enemies hit by the Super that have a damage over time effect. The Hypercharged ants will deal very high damage over time for 1 second, however, they will recharge significantly less Super compared to the normal Super. Trunk also gains a 20% speed boost, as well as a 5% damage and shield boost.

### Tips

*Trunk's short range limits their offensive capabilities, but they have enough range to attack enemies that are at most a tile behind walls, which they can use to their advantage by attacking enemies behind walls. This strictly limits throwers, such as [[Barley]], [[Dynamike]], [[Berry]], [[Willow]], etc. who can otherwise take cover behind walls.
*Trunk can use their main attack to get a brief speed boost, which is especially helpful if an enemy is trying to run away.
*Trunk's Colony Scouts Star Power should generally be used only in bushy maps like [[Cavern Churn]] or [[Feast or Famine]], where it'll greatly improve their performance.
