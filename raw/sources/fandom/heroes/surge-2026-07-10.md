Surge is a {{Rarity|Legendary}} [[Brawler]] who could be unlocked as a [[Brawl Pass]] reward at Tier 30 from Season 2: Summer of Monsters. He has moderate health, a variable attack range, and a high damage potential, but a slow movement and reload speed. He was the 38th Brawler added to the game. His main attack fires a shot of juice that splits upon hitting an enemy. His Super is a jump in the air which allows him to leap over walls, and upon landing, deals damage, knocks enemies back, and grants Surge various upgrades to his attacks and movement speed. Surge's first Gadget, Power Surge, temporarily gives Surge a Super charge rate increase. His second Gadget, Power Shield, partially protects Surge from a single projectile that is converted to reload a part of his ammo. His first Star Power, To the Max!, allows his projectiles to split when hitting a wall. His second Star Power, Serve Ice Cold, lets him start a match with his Super already charged. His Hypercharge, Stage 5, makes Surge upgrade to a fourth stage, firing smaller projectiles upon landing, and giving his shots even more range than Stage 3 and causing them to split every time.

### Attack: Juice of Justice

{{Quote|Surge serves a shot of Juice that splits in two on contact with enemies.}}Surge fires a shot of juice that splits in two at right angles when it hits an enemy. Each of the split shots will deal half the damage and will have half the Super charge of the initial shot. When upgraded to Stage 2 with his Super, Surge's attack range increases. His attack splits into 6 projectiles instead of 2 at Stage 3, with 3 projectiles firing in a wide arc on each side. These split shots continue on their trail for an additional 4 tiles. His attack has a short cooldown and can be fired quickly but reloads slowly.

### Super: Party Tricks

{{Quote|With each Super, Surge gets upgraded (MAX 3). Upgrades are lost when Surge is defeated.<br>STAGE 1: Boosted movement speed<br>STAGE 2: Increased weapon range<br>STAGE 3: Main attack splits into 6 (instead of 2)}}Surge flies up into the air for 0.75 seconds, traveling up to 3.33 tiles forward, knocking enemies back by 2 tiles and dealing damage in a small radius upon landing. While in the air, Surge is completely immune to all damage besides status effects and damage applied over time. In addition, Surge becomes upgraded, and his appearance is altered each time he upgrades. Surge's upgrades reset if he is defeated, a goal is scored in [[Brawl Ball]], or a round is over in [[Knockout]]. Additionally, a chevron will appear beside his health bar that represents his current upgrade stage. This icon can be seen by all players in the game.

### Gadgets / Power Surge

{{Quote|Surge's power surges! Increase Super charge rate by 30% for 5 sec. Cooldown: 15 seconds.}}{{Gadget|1}}Upon activation, Surge's Super charge rate is increased by 30% for 5 seconds.

{{Quote|Power Surge also grants 30% Reload Speed.}}{{Buffie|Gadget}}
With the Buffie, this Gadget also increases Surge's reload speed by 30%.

### Gadgets / Power Shield

{{Quote|Within the next 2 seconds, Surge consumes 50% of the damage of the next incoming hit and uses the energy to reload 2 ammo. Cooldown: 22 seconds.}}{{Gadget|2}}When activated, Surge will gain a special shield that lasts for 2 seconds. The next time he takes damage, he only receives 50% of the normal damage and instantly reloads 2 ammo. Unlike [[Nani]]'s Return to Sender Gadget and [[Surge]]'s Power Shield Gadget, and like [[Kenji]]'s Nigiri Nemesis Star Power, this effect can apply to more than one projectile as long as the damage is done in the same frame (for example, multiple projectiles from [[Shelly]]'s Super). This Gadget's cooldown begins after the button is pressed.

{{Quote|If Surge is hit during Power Shield, Gadget cooldown is decreased by 50%.}}{{Buffie|Gadget}}
With the Buffie, if Surge is hit during the Gadget's activation, the cooldown is decreased by 50%. The reduction is based on the total cooldown, not the remaining cooldown.

With the Buffie, Surge's main attack also splits into two smaller projectiles when bouncing off a wall. ### Star Powers / Serve Ice Cold

{{Quote|Surge always spawns with a full Super Charge.}}{{StarPower|2}}Surge always starts and spawns at Stage 0, but he has a fully charged Super.

{{Quote|While in Stage 3, Surge gains a burst of movement speed when using his Super.}}{{Buffie|Star}}
With the Buffie, after being upgraded to Stage 2+, Surge gains a 50% movement speed boost for 2 seconds.

### Hypercharge: Stage 5

{{Quote|Surge's Super gains increased ranged and shoots Justice Juice in every direction when landing!}}{{Hypercharge}}
When activated, when Surge uses his Super, he upgrades to a fourth stage, giving his shots even more range than Stage 2 or 3, and fires 8 smaller attack projectiles around him. He upgrades to his fourth stage no matter what stage he was at before, but reverts to the stage he would have been at normally after the Hypercharge is over. Additonally, during his Hypercharge's duration, his shots also split regardless if it hits a Brawler or hits a wall with his Star Power To The Max! equipped, even if Surge does not use his Super. Also, his Super jump range is doubled. He also gains a 20% speed boost, as well as a 5% damage and shield boost.

{{Quote|Surge shoots 'Double the Juice' for Double the Justice.}}{{Buffie|Hyper}}
With the Buffie, Surge's attack fires two projectiles in quick succession. The second attack projectile deals 30% less damage than the first, and it also charges his Super 30% less.

### Tips

*Surge does not function well without his first upgrade. Whether or not you have his Serve Ice Cold Star Power, you should charge up your Super as soon as you can and use it to get the speed boost he needs to make up for his rather short range. Don't be hesitant to use his Super as a defensive tool, as without his first upgrade, Surge lacks any offensive ability due to his slow speed and short range. After getting his first upgrade, Supers can either be saved if you're playing against assassins or tanks who could ambush you at close range or used immediately if you're playing against longer-ranged Brawlers who could out-range you, since his second upgrade increases his range.  An effective [[Duo Showdown]] composition would be Surge and [[Bo]] as Bo can drop his Super Totem to help Surge charge his Super in one less hit and allow Surge to get some of his much needed upgrades.
*Surge can momentarily dodge any projectiles approaching him while using his Super. Use his Super at the right moment to avoid a potentially devastating attack; for example, Surge can evade [[Frank]]'s destructive Super by activating his Super right after Frank winds up his hammer. Alternatively, his Super can be used in close-ranged combat as landing his Super on an enemy within a small radius deals damage and also knocks them back. If a close-ranged Brawler is chasing Surge, his Super can be used to physically distance Surge from his enemy and to deter the enemy by dishing out some extra damage.
*Once he reaches Stage 3, Surge's Super shouldn't be used immediately after charging it. It will not upgrade him any further, so it's better to save it until he needs it. It can later be used when he needs to dodge attacks or to escape from a Brawler that gets too close.
**In Showdown, try saving your Super even if you're not at Stage 3. Surge's Super is a vital tool for him to survive in Showdown especially if you're up against assassins and other Brawlers that can get dangerously close to you.
*At his initial stage, Surge is an easy target for throwers. Staying out of range or simply avoiding throwers increases Surge's chance of survival until he reaches Stage 1. After upgrading himself for the first time, Surge can counter their attacks with his very fast movement speed, which quickly turns him into a threat.
*In 3v3 Events, such as [[Brawl Ball]], conserving a charged Super until you respawn can provide a vital speed boost immediately, allowing Surge to easily get back into action. Speed is also required in this mode to score goals; Surge's Serve Ice Cold gives him the speed needed to dodge attacks, get close to enemies, and score goals.
*When using Surge's first gadget Power Surge, your goal should be to get a Super during the time limit as to pump up when the duration is over, as well taking advantage of your new benefit while you can. An example would be to use your speed (If used in base stage) to aggressively pressure opponents.
*Surge's To the Max! Star Power is particularly effective in Showdown. On certain Showdown maps such as [[Rockwall Brawl]] and [[Cavern Churn]], there are long lanes and cramped walls that Surge can utilize to split his shots. To the Max! is extremely powerful if Surge is fully upgraded, as his shots split into 6 instead of 2, making him an immediate threat combined with his high burst damage and Power Surge Gadget. It’s mainly used to hit enemies at walls, and can deal extraordinary damage if all shots, main and split, hit.
**His Serve Ice Cold Star Power is completely useless in Solo Showdown and dubious in Duo Showdown, because it is only activated once Surge is defeated. To the Max! gives better value overall because the split shots from hitting a wall can help aid Surge gain his Super quickly without having to use his Gadgets to gain his pivotal upgrades.
*When fighting another Brawler as Surge, it's extremely important to consider what options they have to counter you.
