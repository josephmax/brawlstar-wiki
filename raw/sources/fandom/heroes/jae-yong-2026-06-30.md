Jae-yong is a {{Rarity|Mythic}} [[Brawler]] who has moderate health, a long range, a variable damage output, a fast movement speed, and the ability to transform into multiple different modes through his Super and Gadgets. Playing as Jae-yong will replace the normal battle music with unique music for whichever mode he is in, unless he is respawning or defeated. He was the 91st Brawler added to the game. His Trait allows his Super to charge itself over time. In his work mode, he attacks with a speaker which drops slipstreams in his path, giving himself and allies a speed boost. In his party mode, he attacks with bottles that damages enemies and heals allies in his path. Jae-yong's Super switches between his party and work modes while giving allies temporary buffs: in work mode, it momentarily boosts the movement speed of his and nearby allies, and, in party mode, it instantly heals him and his allies. His first Gadget, Weekend Warrior, instantly deals damage in a circular area around him and temporarily changes his mode to increase his main attack damage. His second Gadget, Time For a Slow Song, instantly slows enemies in a circular area around him, and temporarily changes his mode to make his attacks slow down enemies. His first Star Power, The Crowd Goes Mild, increases his movement speed for every ally close to him. His second Star Power, Extra High Note, increases his main attack damage for every target it pierces. His Hypercharge, Crowd-Pleaser, reduces the cooldown of his Super, and boosts the Super activation.

### Trait

{{Quote|This Brawler charges Super over time.}}{{Trait|Auto}}Jae-yong's Super automatically charges 15% per second, similar to [[Darryl]], and it takes about 6.67 seconds to fully charge. This Trait doesn't charge his Hypercharge. However, unlike most other Brawlers, he charges an insignificant amount of Super with his attacks.

### Attack: Work Hard, Play Hard

{{Quote|Fires a piercing projectile, with different effects depending on Jae-yong's mode.<br>WORK: Deals less damage, but creates slipstreams with projectiles that give speed boosts to teammates and himself<br>PARTY: Heals teammates with a piercing, damaging projectile.}}In his work mode, Jae-yong throws piercing speakers that deal very low damage and drop trails of slipstreams that follow the attack's path. The slipstreams last for 2.5 seconds each, and increases his and allies' movement speed by 20% for 0.75 seconds on contact. However, they do not affect allies with a spawning invulnerability shield. In his party mode, Jae-yong throws piercing bottles that deal 13.3% more damage than work mode and heal allies in their path for 76.5% of party mode's damage. Both attacks can go through both allies and enemies.

### Super: Mix It Up!

{{Quote|Switch between Jae-yong's Work & Party modes! Switching modes changes Jae-yong's main attack and activates the matching ability.<br>WORK: Gives all nearby teammates a temporary movement speed boost.<br>PARTY: Heals all nearby teammates.}}Jae-yong switches between his work and party modes, which changes his main attack. The attack cooldown is reduced to zero for the next attack when switching modes. When switching into his work mode, he creates a 4.17-tile radius area effect around him that increases the movement speed of his and teammates inside the area-of-effect by 30% for 2 seconds, similar to [[Max]]'s Super. However, it does not affect allies with a spawning invulnerability shield. When switching into his party mode, he instantly heals himself and allies. There is no direction of travel to this Super, so aiming is not required, though aiming can be used to show you the area-of-effect. An icon will appear beside his health bar that represents his current mode. His work mode is represented by a blue double upward-pointing chevron, and his party mode is represented by a green plus sign. This icon can be seen by all players in the game.

Using the Super while either Gadget is active will instantly cancel the effects of that Gadget.

### Gadgets

'''Note:''' When using the Super while the effects of either Gadget are active, the remaining Gadget duration is forfeited and cannot be reinstated.

### Gadgets / Weekend Warrior

{{Quote|Deals area damage and increases main attack damage for 5 sec. Cooldown: 15 seconds.}}{{Gadget|1}}When activated, Jae-yong switches into his damage mode for 5 seconds, and his icon changes into that of an orange fist. The attack cooldown is reduced to zero for the next attack when switching modes. He instantly damages every target in a large 4.17-tile radius, with its damage being equal to the healing from party mode projectiles. The activation damage also charges his Super by 16.25% per hit, but does not charge his Hypercharge. In his damage mode, his main attack is changed into spoons, and the damage is increased to 40% more than work mode or 23.5% more than party mode, but won't drop speed-boosting slipstreams nor heal allies.

### Gadgets / Time For a Slow Song

{{Quote|Jae-yong slows enemies around him and his attacks will leave trails of slowing paperwork for 5 sec. Cooldown: 12 seconds.}}{{Gadget|2}}When activated, Jae-yong switches to his slow mode for 5 seconds, and his icon changes to that of a yellow downward-pointing arrow. The attack cooldown is reduced to zero for the next attack when switching modes. He instantly slows every target in a large 4.17-tile radius by 30% for 2 seconds, and changes his attack to piercing speakers that deal the same damage as his work mode projectiles and drop trails of paperwork that follow the attack's path, but does not drop speed-boosting slipstreams nor heal allies. The paperwork lasts for 3.5 seconds each, and slow enemies down by 30% for 1.25 seconds on contact.

### Star Powers / The Crowd Goes Mild

{{Quote|Being near teammates gives Jae-yong a 10% speed boost per teammate in range.}}{{StarPower|1}}Jae-yong gains a 4.17-tile circle around him. His movement speed is increased by 10% for every nearby ally inside the radius. This speed boost stacks with more allies inside of the radius, and stacks with other speed boosts such as [[Max]]'s Super or [[Stu]]'s Speed Zone Gadget. Ally Jae-yongs will have their radius shown, while enemy Jae-yongs will have their radius hidden.

### Star Powers / Extra High Note

{{Quote|Main Attacks now deal an additional 20% Damage per target pierced.}}{{StarPower|2}}This Star Power increases the attack projectile's damage by 20% for every target it has already hit. This Star Power affects Power Cube boxes in [[Showdown]], allowing for increased damage output if Power Cube boxes align with each other.

### Hypercharge: Crowd-Pleaser

{{Quote|Jae's Super has a reduced cooldown during Hypercharge, and each Super activation is boosted.}}{{Hypercharge}}
When activated, Jae-yong's Super automatically charges 6 times faster, making its cooldown reduced to about 1.11 seconds, and the effects of each Super activated is increased by one third. He also gains a 20% speed boost, as well as a 5% damage and shield boost.

### Tips / Game Modes and Maps

* Jae-yong is a great pick to support teammates in [[Bounty]] and [[Knockout]], giving fundamental boosts to them and being a good counter to commonly found snipers thanks to his speed boost.
* Jae-yong is also a great Brawler in [[Trio Showdown]], being able to keep his teammates alive for long periods of time, which is fundamental in the Game Mode.
* Though he lacks high HP or any way to take the Ball away from opponents holding it, Jae-yong can prove consistent speed boost to whoever on his team is holding the ball in [[Brawl Ball]]. His automatically charged Super additionally allows him to kick the ball with it without any consequence.
* Jae-yong is great at supporting the Gem carriers in [[Gem Grab]], allowing them to heal up if under pressure or run away faster. He can additionally allow the team by starting the game aggressively by using the slipstream from his Work Mode to reach the center of the map quickly.

### Tips / Recommended Build

* Both of Jae-yong's Gadgets find their use, therefore pick carefully based on the match up. Jae-yong's Weekend Warrior Gadget is useful against low HP Brawlers, while his Time for A Slow Song Gadget will help him avoid approaching assassins, who Jae-yong is weak against.
** Jae-yong's Weekend Warrior Gadget can also be used to make up for his low DPS and matches nicely with the speed boost from his Super, or his Extra High Note Star Power.
*Jae-yong's Hypercharge is charged through damage dealt rather than shots hit, meaning a shot paired with his Weekend Warrior Gadget will charge his Hypercharge more than a normal shot.
* Jae-yong's The Crowd Goes Mild Star Power makes Jae-yong harder to kill as he becomes more evasive. As Jae-yong, you want to always stay close to your teammates to support them, and this Star Power benefits that gameplay style.
* Jae-yong has a good synergy with almost all of the [[Gears]].
** The [[Gears|Damage Gear]] helps Jae-yong, as it makes up with his usually low DPS, and has a good synergy with his Weekend Warrior Gadget. However, as Healer you don't wanna stay on low health for long, so if the Damage Gear doesn't fit your playstyle, the [[Gears|Shield Gear]] is another good option due to Jae-yong's average health.
** As a Healer, it is important to often be on the battlefield to support your teammates. Therefore, the [[Gears|Health Gear]] pairs nicely with Jae-yong and helps him fulfill his role better.
** Jae-yong's shots are very wide and easy to hit even from a distance. Therefore, if playing him in a bushy map, the [[Gears|Vision Gear]] pairs nicely with him.

### Tips / Strategies

* At the beginning of the match, Jae-yong can attack forward to boost his and his teammates' movement speeds to control the center or to gain an advantage, such as grabbing Gems early in [[Gem Grab]] or grabbing the Blue Star in [[Bounty]]. Beware of splash damage when using this tactic.
* Jae-yong's Super can be used frequently as it is charged every 6.67 seconds, so do not hesitate to use heal when any teammates are low on health and provide speed buffs when they have enough health to attack.
** However, Jae-yong's Work Mode can be used to help him and teammates escape from close-range combat, so whenever there are assassins on the opponent team, keep an eye on them and swap to Work Mode whenever necessary.
** To that end, use either Gadget, which are both effective in deterring enemies, when being surprised attacked by an opposing assassin.
* A good strategy is to stay Party Mode and support your teammates while all 3 of you attack the enemy team. If the enemy team is forced to retreat, Jae-yong's Super will come immensively helpful by giving your team a speed bost to reach and finish your opponents off.
** However beware that Jae-yong himself will have no way of healing up while attacking, so either poke your opponents from the back or switch to Work Mode and back again to Party Mode as soon as they're available to heal.
* Jae-yong shines only if your teammates are playing Brawlers who actually benefit from your Supers, such as Tanks. Similarly to [[Byron]], Jae-yong pairs well with [[Ash]], being able to support him and heal him while his rage is active. Jae-yong's speed boost additionally works very well with Ash thanks to the speed he receives from his Rage Bar.
