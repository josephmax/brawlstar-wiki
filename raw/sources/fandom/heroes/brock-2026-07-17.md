Brock is a {{Rarity|Rare}} [[Brawler]] who has low health but a high damage output, in exchange for his slow reload speed. He was one of the 15 Brawlers who released at the first Beta launch. His attack shoots long-ranged rockets that explode and deal area damage in a short radius. His Super fires a barrage of rockets in a large area, destroying obstacles. His first Gadget, Rocket Laces, allows him to fire at his feet, damaging nearby enemies and knock them back, and launching Brock into the air. His second Gadget, Rocket Fuel, makes Brock's next attack a mega rocket that has a larger explosion radius, destroys walls and travels faster. His first Star Power, More Rockets!, increases the number of rockets fired from his Super. His second Star Power, Rocket No. Four, increases Brock's ammo capacity to 4. His Hypercharge, Rocket Barrage, increases the number of rockets fired from his next Super, which deal high damage.

### Attack: Rockin' Rocket

{{Quote|Brock lets fly with a single Rockin' Rocket that really goes the distance.}} Brock's attack is a long-ranged rocket missile that deals high area damage in a 1.5-tile splash radius on impact. After exploding, it sets the ground on fire within a 1-tile radius for 2.9 seconds, dealing 30% of the rocket's damage per second. Unlike other marksmen with longer attack cooldowns, Brock's attack has a normal cooldown, however, his attacks reload rather slowly and travel slowly as well.

### Super: Rocket Rain

{{Quote|Brock fires a rocket barrage that takes out enemies and obstacles. He only wants to see you bathing in the Rocket Rain!}}When Brock's Super is used, he launches a volley of 9 rockets at 0.2-second intervals spread out over a large area, dealing heavy damage to any targets hit. These rockets can be fired over obstacles and destroy terrain within a 1.5-tile splash radius, but they can't set the ground on fire. The Super takes 2.05 seconds to complete. Brock can still move while Rocket Rain is in effect, and the rockets fired move along with him. However, he can't attack or reload while using his Super, and it is cancelled if Brock is knocked back, pulled, stunned, or uses his Rocket Laces Gadget while using his Super.

### Gadgets / Rocket Laces

{{Quote|Brock jumps to a targeted area, knocking back nearby enemies on takeoff and landing and dealing minor damage. Cooldown: 20 seconds.}}
{{Gadget|1}}
When used, Brock jumps up to 4 tiles in the aimed direction, making an explosion on takeoff and landing, and allowing him to jump over walls or water. Any enemy in the explosion is knocked back and takes 1000 damage. The explosion has a radius of 2 tiles, and can't destroy walls or bushes. If required to cross over a larger amount of blocks or over lakes, his jumping distance will be increased up to a limit or to the nearest piece of ground. While in the air, Brock is completely immune to all damage besides status effects and damage applied over time. If he uses this Gadget while his Super is active, his Super is cancelled. Auto-aiming this Gadget will aim it towards the nearest enemy, not your direction of movement. This Gadget's cooldown begins after the attack is used.

{{Quote|Hitting an enemy Brawler gives +2 sec movement speed.}}{{Buffie|Gadget}}
With the Buffie, after hitting an enemy Brawler with the Gadget, Brock will also receive 30% movement speed boost for 2 seconds.

### Gadgets / Rocket Fuel

{{Quote|Brock fires a large rocket that destroys terrain and deals damage . Cooldown: 22 seconds.}}
{{Gadget|2}}
When aiming using the Gadget button, Brock shoots a rocket which destroys obstacles and deals 86.21% the damage as one of his main attack rocket. This attack does not deal extra damage and does not have the fire effect. This Gadget's cooldown begins after the attack is used.

### Star Powers / More Rockets!

{{Quote|Rocket Rain now rains down 44% more rockets.}}{{StarPower|1}}Brock's Super now fires 4 more rockets, increasing the number of rockets from 9 to 13. This Star Power also decreases the time between rockets by 25%, but slightly increases the total Super time by 0.2 seconds.

{{Quote|Brock gains 30% movement speed while firing his Super.}}{{Buffie|Star}}
With the Buffie, Brock's movement speed is increased by 30% while using his Super.

### Star Powers / Rocket No. 4

{{Quote|Brock loads a fourth rocket into his launcher, increasing his ammo capacity (and discarding the thermos bottle he's been keeping in his launcher's #4 tube).}}{{StarPower|2}}Brock gains an extra ammo bar, allowing him to have up to 4 attacks ready at once, instead of 3. However, Brock's reload speed remains the same, so it takes 33% longer for him to fully reload all his ammo after firing all four rockets.

{{Quote|Every 6 sec Brock's next attack has increased range.}}{{Buffie|Star}}
With the Buffie, Brock gains a charge bar which takes 6 seconds to charge, and when charged, Brock's next rocket range gets increased by 2 tiles. A radar symbol will glow above Brock's head, along with a sound effect, signaling the charged attack. The aiming reticle will also turn green.

### Hypercharge: Rocket Barrage

{{Quote|Rockets fired during the Super are cluster bombs.}}{{Hypercharge}}
When activated, Brock's next Super fires 4 waves of 7 rockets, each rocket dealing 800 damage, or 840 damage if he has the Hypercharge damage boost. If Brock has his More Rockets Star Power equipped, his Super will fire 5 waves of 7 rockets instead. He also gains a 20% speed boost, a 5% damage boost, and a 5% shield boost.

{{Quote|Rockin' Rockets fires an additional two small rockets, each dealing 60% damage of the main rocket.}}{{Buffie|Hyper}}
With the Buffie, during his Hypercharge, Brock fires two additional small rockets which move perpendicularly and deal 60% damage that of the normal rocket. The Buffie increases Brock's Hypercharge duration by 2 seconds.

### Tips / Game Modes and Maps

*Brock's Super can be used against a wall in front of a goal in [[Brawl Ball]], meaning Brock does not have to go around the wall. He can also use his Rocket Fuel Gadget to break the walls. This applies to all Brawl Ball maps with a wall in front of the goal.
*Use him in [[Heist]] to break the walls covering the enemy safe so that he can land burst damage on the enemy safe easily. Also, with his long range, he can deal massive damage to the safe while staying relatively safe from a safe distance as well. His attack's slow projectile speed will not be a disadvantage as the safe is stationary, but enemies might have time to go to the safe and tank some of the shots.
*Brock isn't a viable Brawler to use in Special Events because he has slow reload speed and his Super can break walls, giving the robots a huge advantage in [[Robo Rumble]] and [[Boss Fight]]. He is also not viable in survival-based modes like Showdown and Hot Zone, as his low health means he is easily ambushed and eliminated.

### Tips / Recommended Build

*Brock's Rocket No. Four is good in [[Knockout]] because you can easily defeat enemies once you have full ammo.
*Brock's Rocket Laces Gadget can be used for briefly getting away from ambushing assassins or tanks. He can also use it to keep distance by jumping over walls or to get close to throwers behind walls.
*Brock can use his Rocket Fuel Gadget to break open important walls on the map without needing to charge his Super - a very important tool in maps such as [[Backyard Bowl]] or [[Shooting Star]]. He can use his Gadget at the start of the match to give his team a huge defensive advantage. One advantage over his Super is that you can better predict the amount of walls Brock's Gadget will destroy as opposed to his Super, which only shows the area-of effect of possible missile hits which is harder to control. However, unlike his Super, this shot can be body-blocked by enemies or spawnables ([[Tara]]'s shadows, [[Ash]]'s rats, [[Mr. P]]'s porters, [[Eve]]'s hatchlings, [[Nita]]'s bear, and [[Charlie]]'s spiders), as the attack does not pierce. However, if your target is standing near their spawnables, the small explosion radius ensures they will still get damaged.
*Brock's Rocket Fuel Gadget aids in Brock's area denial. Brock can deal splash damage, but it is rather difficult unless enemies stay within one tile. Making each individual shot count is vital for Brock whose slow reload speed and projectile speed makes him vulnerable once a couple of shots are fired. It also allows Brock to open up walls on the map without having to charge his Super first.

### Tips / Strategies

*In [[Duo Showdown]], two Brawlers will most likely be near each other, and Brock's rockets have a small area of explosion when it hits something or when the rocket's range distance ends. Shooting a rocket in between the two Brawlers when they're near will damage both of them greatly. Since his attack's splash radius is also large enough to go through walls slightly, use it to damage enemies extremely close to walls, similarly to [[Jacky]], [[Doug]], [[Squeak]], [[Shade]], and [[Hank]]'s attacks.
*Try to predict where opponents are going to be. His rockets move slowly, and the enemies will likely be moving, so shoot to where you think that they will be when your rocket reaches them.
*Firing a single rocket from his Super and then immediately using his Rocket Laces Gadget will cancel his Super. This is especially useful in Brawl Ball when breaking open the goal barrier to make a quick and unexpected score.
*Keep in mind that Brock's rockets explode by themselves if they reach maximum range. You will hit more of your shots if you are standing just barely in range of an enemy, because then the rockets will explode and deal damage even if they don't hit the enemy directly. Avoid staying at a medium distance, as his rockets will become easy to avoid. Missing two or more attacks at that distance means Brock can easily be pushed back, putting him in a disadvantageous position.

### Tips / Other

*Brock performs the best in open maps that don't have a lot of cover so that close-ranged Brawlers can't ambush him.
*Try to keep Brock behind other teammates, because he has fairly low health and is best used for long-ranged support instead.
*Because of Brock's very slow reload time, it's important to make every shot count. Don't fire off all three rockets at once, since that will leave Brock completely defenseless. While his Rocket No. Four Star Power gives Brock one additional ammo, it is still worthwhile to maintain your ammo for when you need it.
*Use Brock's Super whenever the opponents group up or one is hiding behind a wall.
*When using Brock's Super against an enemy, make it count and try to aim it perfectly, as sometimes some Brawlers can escape and deal damage to you when you are still in the duration of the Super.
*Brock's Super is great for getting the enemy team to split up as they avoid the rockets so that teammates can deal with them individually.
*Compared to other long-range Brawlers, Brock has a much shorter cooldown, allowing him to be a threat even when at close range. Because of this, he can easily defeat lower-health Brawlers by quickly firing 3 rockets. Saving Brock's ammunition and using his Gadget Rocket Laces is especially useful for close-range ambushes. This is more effective with his Rocket No. Four Star, allowing him to survive an encounter even against builkier Brawlers.
*Despite being a Marksman, Brock is unable to outperform other Marksmen. This is due to his range being relatively shorter than others of his class, and the low speed of his projectiles hinders his effectiveness at holding his lane. When fighting other ranged brawlers like Piper, Belle, and Mandy, try to avoid being hit by two or more shots as much as possible, and use your quick unload to put on the pressure. The rockets' burning area and blast radius limit enemy movement, allowing you to fire a rocket on the side they are moving from and then fire in the opposite direction, causing them to be hit by the rockets inadvertently. It's advisable to use the Shield Gear on open maps against other Marksmen, as the extra health means these brawlers will need one more attack to eliminate you, which can be crucial to winning a match. The Rocket Fuel Gadget can help at finishing enemies with low health at a safe distance.
