{{Quote|Alli is the shy mechanic of the Love Swamp. She loves to get her hands dirty, by either fixing broken machines or... mercilessly stalking other Brawlers. If you're walking home late at night and hear an odd noise, it's probably Alli trying to say hello.}}
{{Portrait}}
Alli is a {{Rarity|Mythic}} [[Brawler]] who has moderately high health and damage output, great mobility with her fast movement speed and her attacks, and a variable attack range and reload speed. She was the 93rd Brawler added to the game. Alli's Traits allow her to move over water, and to become enraged, gaining a temporary movement speed boost and an attack range buff when nearby enemies with less than 50% health. Her attack is a short dash on the ground; in bushes and over water, she attacks by jumping over obstacles, dealing damage to all enemies in a small radius when she lands. For her Super, she becomes inconsistently invisible, and her first attack deals additional damage proportional to the enemy's health. Her first Gadget, Feed the Gators, allows her next attack to also heal herself proportional to her attack's damage. Her second Gadget, Cold-Blooded, allows Alli to become temporarily enraged. Her first Star Power, Lizard Limbs, allows her to regenerate health sooner than normal while being enraged. Her second Star Power, You Better Run, You Better Take Cover, increases Alli's reload speed while being enraged. Her Hypercharge, Swamp Snacking, makes her shots heal her when she hits enemies.

### Traits / Water

{{Quote|This Brawler can move over water.}}{{Trait|Water}}
Alli's first Trait allows her to be able to move on water.

### Traits / Enraged

{{Quote|This Brawler becomes enraged and gains movement speed when tracking an enemy with less than 50% health.}}{{Trait|Enraged}}
Alli's other Trait allows her to become enraged when at least 1 enemy with 50% of their maximum health or less is located on screen. While enraged, angry particles will emit from her, and Alli moves 30% faster when she is moving in the general direction of the enemy. Pairs of glowing blue eyes reveal the locations of nearby enemies and pulsing footprint trails show the most direct path to them to Alli, even those in bushes. Enemies will see a single blue eye on their ammo bar signifying that they are activating her enrage. When enemy Brawlers with 50% of their maximum health or less move out of range or begin healing above the 50% threshold, the enrage persists for another 5 seconds, but if they are defeated instead, the enrage disappears immediately.

### Attack: Swamp Swipe

{{Quote|Alli dashes forward, swiping as she goes. When in a bush or water, Alli will leap forward instead.}}On the ground, Alli dashes forward a short distance, similar to [[Mortis]] and [[Kenji]], dealing moderately high damage to any enemy Brawlers in her path. Alli can't perform her attack without dashing unless she dashes toward a nearby wall, where her dash is cut short. This is technically a melee attack because Alli has to dash directly onto the enemy to damage them and the dash has a short range.

When in bushes or swimming in water, she instead attacks by jumping a short distance, similar to [[Mico]]. Her ammo bar becomes yellow instead of the usual orange, and she reloads slower. She stays airborne for approximately 0.33 seconds every jump. Upon landing, she deals moderately high damage in a 1.5-tile radius. If Alli lands on an obstacle, she will automatically be pushed to the closest ground (this is despite her being able to move in water). While in the air, Alli is completely immune to all damage besides status effects and damage applied over time.

Both attacks deal the same amount of damage and have a short cooldown. The attack range of both attacks is roughly proportional to her movement speed, so the range will be increased while she is enraged or has a speed buff such as from the Speed [[Gears|Gear]], and decreased if she is slowed. Every 250 points of speed will affect the dash range by 0.67 tiles and the jump range by one tile.

### Super: Stalker

{{Quote|Alli gains stealth (pulsing invisibility). Attacking does not break stealth. The next main attack she does will deal increased damage based on the enemy's current health percent. The higher their current health, the more damage Alli will deal!}}
[[File:Visibility Warning.png|left|40x40px|link=]]
Alli turns herself invisible for 7 seconds, allowing her to either retreat or ambush an enemy. Unlike most invisibility abilities, Alli continues to stay invisible even after attacking; however, she reveals herself for a brief duration twice during the Super duration, more specifically, 0.5 seconds of visibility alternating with 2 seconds of invisibility. Enemy Brawlers can also see her if she is within 4 tiles of them, and a symbol is displayed left of your health bar if they detect you.

In addition, using her Super alters her next main attack to deal bonus damage. The damage boost is equal to 25% of the current health of the enemy; thus she can do a large amount of damage to high-health targets such as [[Frank]], [[Draco]], or [[El Primo]], but low damage to low-health targets such as [[Piper]], [[Meg]], or [[Tick]]. [[Showdown|Power Cube]] buffs are applied after the base damage is calculated. The bonus damage has a certain minimum and maximum amount of damage it can do, with the maximum amount being equal to her attack damage. For special targets (such as [[Showdown|Power Cube]] Boxes, [[Heist]] safes, spawnables, Big Brawlers in [[Big Game]] or other non-Brawlers), the boosted attack will deal maximum bonus damage. This is affected by outside buffs such as Power Cubes and [[8-Bit]]'s damage booster.

Missing the next attack wastes the extra damage, so it is crucial to actually land the next attack after a Super, though the boosted attack does not expire after the 7 seconds invisibility expires.

### Gadgets / Feed the Gators

{{Quote|The next main attack heals Alli for 100% of the damage dealt. Cooldown: 10 seconds.}}{{Gadget|1}}
Activating this Gadget allows Alli's next attack to also heal her for the amount of damage it does. A Gadget symbol will glow above Alli's head signaling this Gadget's activation, as well as a glowing attack joystick. This Gadget's cooldown begins after the attack is used.

### Gadgets / Cold-Blooded

{{Quote|Alli becomes enraged for the next 4 secs. Can only be activated when an enemy is visible. Cooldown: 17 seconds.}}{{Gadget|2}}
Activating this Gadget allows Alli to trigger her enrage, even if the enemies don't necessarily meet the enrage threshold. This Gadget does not require enemies to be visible to be activated, but it does require it to take effect. This Gadget's cooldown begins after the duration ends.

### Star Powers / Lizard Limbs

{{Quote|While enraged Alli regenerates health faster out of combat after only 1.5 seconds.}}{{StarPower|1}}
While enraged, Alli's auto-regeneration now starts 1.5 seconds after being damaged or attacking instead of the usual 3 seconds.

### Star Powers / You Better Run, You Better Take Cover

{{Quote|Alli reloads 25% faster while enraged.}}{{StarPower|2}}
While enraged, Alli's attack reload speed is increased by 25%.

### Hypercharge: Swamp Snacking

{{Quote|Alli's Super damages for 25% of enemies max health. Damaging enemies heals Alli for 20% of damage dealt.}}{{Hypercharge}}
When activated, the bonus damage from Alli's Super is now 25% of an enemy's maximum health, not their current health. In addition, during the Hypercharge, Alli's attack will heal for 20% of the damage dealt by her attacks, even if she does not use her Super. Alli also gains a 20% speed boost, as well as a 5% damage and shield boost. In addition, the Hypercharge damage boost increases her Super's minimum and maximum bonus damage, as well as her special target damage.

### Tips

*Keep in mind that Alli can only jump when in bushes or underwater, making most jumps a one-way trip. As such, be careful of which target you select, as you may not be able to escape if things get hairy.
*Alli's attack gimmick allows her to jump over enemy attacks, making her useful against burst assassins such as [[Edgar]] and [[Kenji]] who tend to unload all their ammo at once. As such, when playing against her with these Brawlers, try to conserve ammo as Alli's reload speed afterwards is incredibly slow and allows for easy retaliation.
*Alli's slow reload speed prevents her from dealing with hordes of enemies or escaping from them. Thus, she excels in the beginning of modes such as [[Solo Showdown]], where she can target weaker Brawlers before teaming occurs or they begin to gather more [[Showdown|Power Cubes]].
**If possible, try to reload outside of bushes or water as reload speed is slower while in these regions.
*In game modes such as [[Gem Grab]], try to linger nearer to the opposing side of the field. Opponents will often try to retreat back there when on low health, allowing her to ambush them quickly and efficiently.
*As Alli's dash range is reliant on her speed, try to equip the Speed Gear to boost it even further.
*In a pinch, Alli's Super can be used for an extra burst of damage against bulkier targets. This should only be used in desperate circumstances however, as she's heavily reliant on it to approach future targets thanks to her movement speed being slower than most other Assassins. 
*Pay attention to Alli's Enrage counter: the counter decreasing means the enemy has begun healing, while the counter running out means that the threshold has been crossed already.
*Try to use your Super when the enemy team is huddled up together: the burst damage will allow you to take them all down quickly or at least make the cleanup much easier for your teammates.
*If you are using the Feed the Gators Gadget, try to hit multiple enemies and attack while the Super damage bonus is active so that your healing amount is increased.
*Use your Cold Blooded Gadget in conjunction with your Super, especially when enemies flee or you've just respawned in. This allows you to close the distance much faster and get back into the fray.
*Ideally, attack Brawlers that are currently in bushes or in water. These situations allow you to dodge projectiles and deal damage faster, and, in a pinch, quickly leap out of a tough situation.
*Try and stay near a wall that is next to either a patch of grass or water. This is her best battle-ground and allows her to play around with her jumps better.
*Owing to her worse than usual reload and unload speed, every bit of Alli's ammo is precious, so try not to autoaim unless absolutely sure your attacks will hit.
