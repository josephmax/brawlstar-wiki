{{Quote|Mico is the sound guy in Brawlywood, but one could be forgiven for thinking he's actually a celebrity given all the bragging and name-dropping. Hot-tempered and unstable, he cannot take a joke. Don't ever call him funny...}}{{Portrait}}
Mico is a {{Rarity|Mythic}} [[Brawler]] who could be unlocked for free as a Brawlidays 2023 gift from December 14th through December 31st. He has moderate health and damage output, a very fast movement speed, a short attack range, and immense utility with his attack and Super, but suffers by having a very slow reload speed. He was the 74th Brawler added to the game. He attacks by jumping over obstacles, dealing damage to any enemies in a small radius when he lands. His Super is a jump that, upon landing, deals damage and knocks enemies back. His first Gadget, Clipping Scream, fires 3 long-ranged projectiles that slow down and damage enemies on impact. His second Gadget, Presto, increases the range of his next attack. His first Star Power, Monkey Business, periodically makes his next attack remove ammo from enemy Brawlers hit while restoring Mico's ammo. His second Star Power, Record Smash, doubles the damage on special targets. His Hypercharge, Sound Check, makes Mico's next Super stun enemies.

### Attack: Mic BOOM!

{{Quote|Mico jumps forward for a short distance attacking with his mic boom and damaging nearby enemies after landing. }}Mico attacks by jumping a short distance, staying airborne for approximately 0.58 seconds, regardless of the distance traveled. Upon landing, he deals moderate damage in a 1.5-tile radius. If required to cross over a larger amount of blocks or over lakes, his jumping distance will be increased up to a limit or to the nearest piece of ground. While in the air, Mico is completely immune to all damage besides status effects and damage applied over time. Mico's attack has a slow cooldown and a very slow reload speed.

### Super: Out of Frame

{{Quote|Mico jumps out of frame, becoming invulnerable while controlling his landing position and damaging nearby enemies while landing.}}After a 1-second delay, Mico jumps in the air for 6 seconds, dealing damage and knocking enemies back in a 2.33-tile radius upon landing. Where he will land is highlighted with an area-of-effect that gradually increases in size over time. While in the air, Mico can reload and heal, and is completely immune to all damage besides status effects and damage applied over time. However, if hit with a stun, pull, or knockback during the delay, his Super is cancelled. His Super recharge rate scales based on the damage done, meaning it is increased with Power Levels as well as by damage buffs and debuffs.

### Gadgets / Clipping Scream

{{Quote|Mico lets out a deafening scream towards the nearest enemy dealing 570 damage and slowing enemies down for 2 seconds on hit. Cooldown: 15 seconds.}}{{Gadget|1}}Mico fires three long-ranged projectiles with a slight spread. These projectiles deal 25% of the attack damage to enemies upon contact, while also slowing them down by 350 points for 2 seconds. This Gadget can only be used if there is an enemy in a 9-tile radius, and its cooldown begins 0.25 seconds after it's activated.

### Gadgets / Presto

{{Quote|Mico's next jump has 34% longer range. Cooldown: 14 seconds.}}{{Gadget|2}}
Upon activation, Mico's next attack range is increased by 1.33 tiles. The attack joystick will glow signaling this Gadget's activation. This Gadget's cooldown begins after the attack is used.

### Star Powers / Monkey Business

{{Quote|Every 5 seconds, Mico's basic attack steals ammo from his enemies after hitting them.}}{{StarPower|1}}
Mico gains a bar that takes 5 seconds to charge up, and when fully charged, enemies hit by Mico's next attack will lose one-third of their ammo. Meanwhile, Mico gains the stolen ammo to his own ammo bar. If an enemy Brawler's ammo bar is empty, Mico will not receive any more ammo even if the attack hits them, but Mico can still remove enemies' ammo even if his ammo bar is full. This Star Power does not work against the [[Heist]] safe or spawnables. However, the bar resets if Mico doesn't hit a target.

### Star Powers / Record Smash

{{Quote|Mico deals 100% extra damage against non-Brawlers.}}{{StarPower|2}}
This Star Power doubles Mico's attack damage to every special target, such as [[Nita]]'s bear, [[Jessie]], [[Pam]], and [[8-Bit]]'s turrets, [[Penny]]'s mortar, [[Mr. P]]'s porter, [[Larry & Lawrie|Lawrie]], [[Showdown]] boxes, and [[Heist]] safe.

### Hypercharge: Sound Check

{{Quote|Mico's Super also stuns nearby enemies after landing for 1.5 seconds.}}{{Hypercharge}}
When activated, Mico's next Super stuns enemies hit by its impact damage for 1.5 seconds. He also gains a 20% speed boost, as well as a 5% damage and shield boost.

### Tips

*Use your jumps sparingly. Mico's reload speed is very slow, so avoid wasting too much ammo while using your attack for mobility. Try not to burst down all your ammo, and instead time your attacks with those of your target's.
*To optimise the usage of Clipping Scream Gadget, jump directly onto opponents before using it, ensuring that all 3 projectiles hit. While this Gadget's slow should buy you time to escape, ensure to keep at least 1 ammo to escape.
*You can also use Mico's Clipping Scream to slow down melee Brawlers trying to chase you down, allowing you to not have to use his main attack to jump away.
*When engaging other Micos, it may be better to auto-aim right on top of them to deal damage. This is because many Mico confrontations will likely result in both parties trying to attack each other simultaneously and as such, it may be better to dodge at the last second and attack your opponent.
*Keep in mind that Mico's Super slows down as it locks on, so try to corner Brawlers you target before you strike.
* Mico's health is not extremely high and the 1 second delay to execute his Super leaves him vulnerable to defeat. As such, take advantage of the 6 second delay and use your Super behind cover before tracking your target.
*Thanks to Mico's long airtime during his Super, he can use it similarly to [[Janet]]'s Super in Gem Grab, escaping from opponents during the countdown as well as recovering in the air.
*When engaging assassins, ensure you have at least one ammo to disengage. This is because, Mico lacks any real options for dealing high burst damage or recovering health, meaning that without his Clipping Scream Gadget, Mico cannot escape effectively and will likely be defeated.
*Thanks to Mico's small damage radius, train to anticipate enemy movement, corner enemies into smaller bundles or use a more ambush-oriented fighting style. The latter style is best paired with his Monkey Business Star Power as it prevents Brawlers with slow reload from fighting back, especially after confronting other Brawlers.
*Thanks to the long time in the air that Mico's Super possesses, using the Hypercharge and immediately using your Super would cause the Hypercharge to be wasted. The Hypercharged Super is less impactful in relation to other Hypercharges, so using the Hypercharge as a pure stat buff for attacks is a good idea. Landing on an opponent, and then activating Hypercharge to quickly defeat them is a good strategy.
*Unlike other Brawlers with similar attack-boosting bars, Mico's Monkey Business bar recharges regardless of whether he’s attacking or not. As such, it’s recommended for Mico players to attack as soon as possible their bar is full, allowing for possible team wipes when Mico recharges during his attack.
*Mico's attack damage is done a frame before the jump ends, making him immune to immediate damage retaliation from the likes of [[Jacky]]'s Counter Crush, [[Nani]]'s Return to Sender, or [[Meg]]'s Heavy Metal Star Power. This applies to knockbacks as well, meaning Mico can damage enemies attempting to hide inside [[Gale]]'s Twister Gadget.
*Similar to [[Buzz]]'s Reserve Buoy Gadget, Presto can be used as a kill confirm option to counteract Mico's short range more effectively than Clipping Scream due to Mico's main attack dealing more damage. Unlike most Gadgets that buff a Brawlers main attack however, Presto does not show a Gadget symbol above him, making it a great surprise option at practically any time.
*Using the Record Smash Star Power, Mico can deal immense amounts of damage to the [[Heist]] safe and Power Cube boxes.
*If you are going to use Monkey Business Star Power, use one attack just before the bar fills so you can approach the enemy and activate your ammo stealing ability, which makes up the ammo you used to approach the enemy. Alternatively, you can use Presto Gadget.
*Don't go against enemies that can deal constant damage with very fast unload speed such as [[Carl]] or [[Gigi]], because they deal damage whenever someone is near them using their Super and attack respectively, so it's impossible to dodge their attacks using the jumping feature because they will damage whenever Mico is on the ground.
