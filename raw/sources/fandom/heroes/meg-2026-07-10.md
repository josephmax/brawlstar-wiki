Meg is a {{Rarity|Legendary}} [[Brawler]] who has moderate health and high damage in her advanced form but can attain her alternate form that has very low damage and health. Meg has a very fast movement speed in her base form and a very long-ranged attack in both forms. She was the 51st Brawler added to the game. Meg attacks by firing two bolts in quick succession that deal low damage to enemies. Her Super transforms her into a Mecha that momentarily increases her health and modifies Meg's attack and Super, damaging and knocking nearby enemies in the process. The Mecha's attack fires two streams of bolts, while its Super deals high damage in a wide arc. Her first Gadget, Jolting Volts, momentarily heals her Mecha and her nearby allies while it is active. Her second Gadget, Toolbox, fires her Mecha in the direction Meg's facing, and damaging enemies along the way and on impact, while also automatically charging her Super overtime. Her first Star Power, Force Field, grants her a long-lasting damage reduction shield after her Mecha is taken out. Her second Star Power, Heavy Metal, heals her Mecha after using the Super. Her Hypercharge, Tungsten Toughness, increases the range of her Mecha's Super, or temporarily slow the nearby targets after she creates her Mecha.

### Attacks / Basic: Target Acquired

{{Quote|Meg fires a short burst from her blaster that pokes at her enemies' patience.}}Meg fires two long-ranged bolts in quick succession that deal low damage to enemies. This attack has a very fast unload speed, taking only 0.4 seconds to complete.

### Attacks / Advanced: Crowd Control

{{Quote|A strong barrage of bullets that leaves nothing to chance. It's the quickest way to make crowds disperse!}}Meg's Mecha fires 16 bolts arranged in two staggered streams, which have a cone-shaped spread. This attack has a slow unload speed, taking 1 second to complete.

### Supers / Basic: Mecha Machina

{{Quote|Meg jumps into her Mecha for some colossal carnage! On landing, mecha damages nearby opponents by 3000.}}Meg's Super creates a Mecha after a 1-second delay, damaging and knocking nearby enemies back in the process. The Mecha has its own health and modifies Meg's attack and Super. However, Meg's movement speed, unload speed and range are reduced while she's controlling it. Meg's Super transformation can be cancelled if she is stunned, knocked back, or pulled, and she is unable to attack or move during the 1-second delay. If Meg is taken out, her Super charge will go to her Mech when she respawns.

### Supers / Advanced: Feel the Steel

{{Quote|The Mecha swings a formidable forearm that enemies will see and feel.}}The Mecha's Super is a secondary attack that is a wide, piercing swing that deals high damage after a 0.2-second delay, similarly to [[Bibi]]'s attack. If her Mecha is taken out while its Super is charged, Meg won't have its Super when she transforms back.

### Gadgets / Jolting Volts

{{Quote|Heal the mecha by 518 per second for 5 seconds. Can only be used in mecha form. Cooldown: 17 seconds.}}{{Gadget|1}}This Gadget heals Meg's Mecha heals for 7% of its maximum health per second for 5 seconds. This Gadget cannot be activated if her Mecha is not active. This Gadget's cooldown begins after the effect ends.

{{Quote|Jolting Volts heals for +1 sec.}}{{Buffie|Gadget}}
With the Buffie, Jolting Volts will now also heal Meg's Mecha & nearby teammates for 11% of their maximum health per second. The healing radius is centered on Meg and moves with her. In addition, the healing duration is increased from 5 to 6 seconds.

Cooldown: 22 seconds.}}{{Gadget|2}}Meg immediately ejects from the Mecha and launches it forward, dealing damage both when firing the Mecha and when it explodes. With the Buffie, the Mecha also leaves behind a trail of fire that deals 30% of the launched Mecha damage for 3 seconds. Enemies who touch it are set on fire to burn while they stand in the trail and for 1 second after they leave.

### Star Powers / Force Field

{{Quote|Meg is protected by a 40% shield for 5 seconds when her Mecha is destroyed.}}{{StarPower|1}}This Star Power grants Meg a 40% shield for 5 seconds after her Mecha is taken out. The shield duration is cut short if Meg uses her Super again.

{{Quote|Meg gains +20% movement speed while the shield is active.}}{{Buffie|Star}}
With the Buffie, Meg's movement speed is also increased by 20%, while her shield is active.

### Star Powers / Heavy Metal

{{Quote|The Mecha recovers 1200 health for each enemy hit with Feel the Steel (Super in Mech).}}{{StarPower|2}}When the Mecha uses its Super, it heals for 70.59% of the damage dealt from every enemy hit. The healing stacks when more enemies are hit.

{{Quote|Feel the Steel also steals 1 Ammo from enemy Brawlers. More like Feel the Steal, right??}}{{Buffie|Star}}
With the Buffie, enemies hit by the Mecha's swing will lose one ammo. Meanwhile, the Mecha gains the stolen ammo to its own ammo bar. If an enemy Brawler's ammo bar is empty, the Mecha will not receive any more ammo even if the swing hits them, but the Mecha can still remove enemies' ammo even if his ammo bar is full. This Buffie does not work against the [[Heist]] safe or spawnables.

### Hypercharge: Tungsten Toughness

{{Quote|Feel the Steel has increased range, and enemies hit when Meg deploys the Mecha are slowed for 1 sec.}}{{Hypercharge}}
When activated, both of Meg's Supers get a buff depending on what form she is in when she uses the Super.

While in the Mecha, the Super's range is increased by 2.33 tiles. While outside the Mecha, when she enters it, the Mecha slows the nearby targets for 1 second. Meg also gains a 20% speed boost, and a 5% damage and shield boost.

{{Quote|The Mecha's Main Attack explodes, dealing 400 area damage. Outside her Mecha, Meg's Main Attack homes to enemies and fires an extra projectile.}}{{Buffie|Hyper}}
With the Buffie, while in the Mecha's attacks explode on impact in a small radius, dealing 400 damage each. While outside the Mecha, Meg fires 3 projectiles instead of 2 that will curve towards the nearest enemy.

### Tips

*Meg is a dependable damage dealer when in her Mecha, making her particularly effective at dealing damage to the [[Heist]] safe or applying pressure in [[Duels]]. However, her reload speed and movement speed are reduced while in the Mecha. As a result, it’s advisable to avoid chasing down enemies in modes like [[Bounty]] or [[Knockout]].
*Meg is an agile poker without her Mecha because of her fast movement speed and fast unload speed, but with it, she becomes a devastating fighter and control asset as she can deal high damage in large areas with her attack and secondary Super.
*There's a slight delay before Meg hops into her Mecha, and her Super can be cancelled if she dies, gets knocked back, or gets stunned. Make sure to activate her Super where you'll be safe from attacking enemies.
*In [[Brawl Ball]], Meg can be very viable as a defense to oncoming opponents with the ball as her Mecha has a lot of health and can be used to significantly damage opponents. She can take enemy fire and provide some area denial to help the ball carrier score an easy goal.
*In [[Showdown]], Meg can be a force to be reckoned with due to her starting in her much stronger Mecha form. Combined with her Jolting Volts Gadget, Meg can duel even longer range Brawlers due to the Mecha's high health. However, Meg becomes much weaker after it is destroyed. Prioritize picking up Power Cubes and use her Force Field Star Power to increase Meg's survivability outside her Mecha.
*When the various upgrades the Mecha gives Meg are needed more than usual, using Meg's Jolting Volts Gadget can keep Meg in her Mecha significantly longer against long range Brawlers that can prevent it from healing. This is useful in Heist because Meg will have a very low impact on the health of the Safe in her standard form, but can deal substantial damage onto the safe when inside her Mecha.
*Be mindful when taking on high-responsibility roles such as the Gem carrier in [[Gem Grab]] or the ball carrier in Brawl Ball, because if Meg's Mecha is taken out, she will become an easy target.
*Avoid close combat and engaging with aggressive Brawlers after her Mecha is destroyed. Either Star Power can help by shielding some damage or pushing away enemies near Meg, but those won't completely save her from taking damage or being defeated.
*Meg's advanced Super will cancel its main attack. Keep this in mind if you want to deal substantial long-range damage.
*In [[Ranked]] games with the Big Friend [[Modifiers|Modifier]], both Meg and her Mecha will have their health scaled up. This makes her particularly difficult to fully take down and allows her to more easily recharge her Mecha once destroyed.
*In her Mecha form, Meg is able to deal a lot of damage and keep control of an area with her high-damaging attack and Super. This makes her an extreme menace in modes like [[Hot Zone]] and [[Brawl Ball]]. Since her range is shorter than most long-range Brawlers, she excels in maps that require mid-lane control.
