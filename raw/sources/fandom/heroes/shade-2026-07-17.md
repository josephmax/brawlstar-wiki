Shade is an {{Rarity|Epic}} [[Brawler]] who has a short attack range, moderate health, a very fast movement and reload speed, a variable damage output, and immense utility with its attack and Super. It was the 86th Brawler added to the game. Its Traits allow Shade to passively charge its Super if an enemy Brawler is within a radius of it, and move over water. Shade attacks by hugging in a wide and close-ranged arc, that deals double damage in the center. For its Super, it dashes forward and momentarily passes through obstacles. Its first Gadget, Longarms, increases the range of its next attack. Its second Gadget, Jump Scare, temporarily slows enemies down in small radius around Shade. Its first Star Power, Spooky Speedster, temporarily increases Shade's movement when it hits an enemy in the center of its attacks. Its second Star Power, Hardened Hoodie, reduces incoming damage for the duration of its Super. Its Hypercharge, The Frightener, adds double the damage to Shade's attack for the duration of its Super.

### Traits / Radius

{{Quote|This Brawler charges Super from staying close to opposing enemies.}}{{Trait|Radius}}
Shade's first Trait is a 6-tile circle around it. Similar to [[Buzz]], [[Cordelius]], and [[Lily]], all enemy Brawlers within this circle charge Shade's Super. It charges its Super and Hypercharge faster if there are multiple enemies inside the radius, and the amount of Super charge per second is 3.375% multiplied by one more than the number of enemies present within the circle. This results in the Super charging by 6.75% per second each or over 15 seconds for one enemy, and its Hypercharge by 2.7% per second each or over 37 seconds for one enemy. Its Super and Hypercharge still charge even with invisible or bush-camping enemies. Ally Shades will have their Trait radius shown, while enemy Shades will have their Trait radius hidden.

### Traits / Water

{{Quote|This Brawler can move over water.}}{{Trait|Water}}
Shade's other Trait allows it to be able to move on water.

### Attack: Haunted Hug

{{Quote|Shade hugs its opponents with a wide embrace, accidentally dealing damage, and doing more damage to those caught in the center of the hug. It can also hug enemies through walls!}}
Shade swings its arms, hugging enemies in a wide, 135-degree arc, including enemies behind walls. The attack, much like [[Frank]]'s, [[Bibi]]'s, and [[Ash]]'s, takes time to wind up, at 0.5 seconds. However, Shade isn't immobile while attacking and there isn't any travel time, so the attack instantly takes effect after the delay. In addition, if enemies are in the center of Shade's attacks, it doubles the damage. If Shade is hit with a stun, pull, or knockback during the delay, its attack is cancelled, but if it happens during the first 0.35 seconds of the delay, its ammo is retained.

### Super: Incorporeal Form

{{Quote|Shade leaves the physical world and can move directly through the environment. It also gains a movement speed boost.}}
Shade's Super is a short dash that travels through obstacles. In addition, Shade gains 30% movement speed and the ability to pass through obstacles for 6 seconds, along with an altered appearance. The 6-second timer starts at the beginning of the dash. After 6 seconds, Shade transforms back to normal. If Shade is in an obstacle when its Super ends, it will automatically be pushed to the closest piece of ground (despite being able to go over water). If while its Super is active Shade uses another, the duration of the Super will refresh.

### Gadgets / Longarms

{{Quote|The next main attack has 50% longer range. Cooldown: 15 seconds.}}{{Gadget|1}}
Upon activation, Shade's next attack range is increased by 1.67 tiles. A Gadget symbol will glow above Shade's head signaling this Gadget's activation, as well as a glowing attack joystick. This Gadget's cooldown begins after the attack is used.

### Gadgets / Jump Scare

{{Quote|Shade spooks nearby enemies, slowing them for 4 seconds. Cooldown: 9 seconds.}}{{Gadget|2}}
Shade creates a 3.33-tile radius of ghosts around it that slows enemies by 20% for 4 seconds.

### Star Powers / Spooky Speedster

{{Quote|Hitting opponents with the center of Shade's main attack gives a short movement speed.}}{{StarPower|1}}
When Shade hits an enemy in the center of its hugs, it gains a 15% movement speed buff for 1 second. The speed buff stacks if Shade hits more enemies in the center.

### Star Powers / Hardened Hoodie

{{Quote|Shade gets 30% damage reduction while in its Incorporeal Form.}}{{StarPower|2}}
Shade gains a shield that reduces all damage it takes by 30% for the duration of its Super.

### Hypercharge: The Frightener

{{Quote|Shade's attacks during its Hypercharged Super all deal deal maximum damage, even if the enemy is not hugged in the center.}}{{Hypercharge}}
When activated, if Shade uses its Super for the duration of the Hypercharge, its main attack now deals double the damage in any part. However, if the Hypercharged Super is active after the Hypercharge is over, the main attack damage returns to normal. Shade also gains a 20% speed boost, as well as a 5% damage and shield boost.

### Tips / Game Modes and Maps

* Shade is viable in most of [[Brawl Arena]] as its Radius trait is fully functional on bots. This allows for consistent Super cycling.
** Especially, [[The Smackdome]] favors Shade heavily due to the wide distribution of unbreakable walls. On the other hand, [[Kaiju Lake]], with its relatively open terrain, tends to be one of Shade's weaker maps.
* It also has a niche on [[Parallel Plays]] in [[Hot Zone]] as a result of its ability to reach beyond walls. Its Super also makes Shade hard to attack if used into a wall. However, it might struggle initially against other ranged options on this map because of its lack of range, such as [[Sirius]], if Shade is unable to charge a Super.
* In a light of its ability to attack through obstacles and its short range, Shade is terrifically durable in closed maps with a lot of walls as it can stay behind walls from time to time to elude from most enemies' attacks while consistently attacking them without being touched.

### Tips / Recommended Build

* Longarms is the better Gadget to use all the time, as it helps fix Shade's biggest weakness - lack of range - and allows you to charge your Super faster from a safe distance. Jump Scare's radius is very small, and enemies within the range of the slow are likely close enough that you can chase and hit them without it anyways.
*  Hardened Hoodie is usually the preferred Star Power to use, as it provides additional protection to Shade when it needs it most. The speed boost from Spooky Speedster is nice, however, and can be used if you are confident in hitting the center attack.
*Shield and Damage Gears are the best gears for Shade overall, although Speed Gear could be used on more bushy maps to exaggerate Shade's already fast speed.

### Tips / Other

* The best way to use Shade's Super is to pressure enemies by dashing into and hiding in a wall nearby them. Most enemies are unable to hit Shade for as long as it stands in the wall, allowing you to freely damage and push them back. However, be wary of enemies that can outrange you and still hit you, namely throwers (except [[Sprout]] and [[Grom]]), [[Mr. P]], [[Hank]], [[Doug]], [[Trunk]], [[Brock]], [[Mico]], and [[Jacky]].
* When attacking through walls, make sure to monitor Shade's remaining Super duration, as if it ends in a bad position, Shade may be pushed into the enemies you were just attacking, essentially guaranteeing death. As your Super ends, make sure you either use it again (if you were able to charge it) or retreat to safety.
