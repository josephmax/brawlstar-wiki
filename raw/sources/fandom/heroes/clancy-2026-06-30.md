Clancy is a {{Rarity|Mythic}} [[Brawler]] who has moderate health, a long range, and a variable damage output, but a slow reload speed. His Trait allows him to gather tokens and upgrade his attack and Super during a match. He was the 82nd Brawler added to the game. Clancy attacks by shooting bullets, with the attack pattern and damage changing depending on his Stage. His Super fires a barrage of projectiles in a fan shape, which increases in range and damage depending on his current Stage. His first Gadget, Snappy Shooting, allows him to obtain his upgrades faster in a short duration. His second Gadget, Tactical Retreat, allows him to dash a few tiles in the direction he is facing and reload one ammo. His first Star Power, Recon, gives him some tokens at the start of a match. His second Star Power, Pumping Up, reloads all his ammo when he defeats an enemy. His Hypercharge, Recall of Duty, makes his Super's paintballs pierce through enemies and return to him once they have reached max range.

### Trait

{{Quote|Clancy gains tokens when his abilities hit enemy Brawlers!}}{{Trait|Tokens}}
Clancy obtains tokens for every projectile he successfully hits with his main attack or Super. It takes 6 hits for him to level up from Stage 1 to Stage 2, and 14 hits from Stage 2 to Stage 3. Clancy also moves 100 points faster when he reaches Stage 3. Even if he is defeated, he will keep the Stage he was in after he respawns.

### Attack: Power Wash

{{Quote|Clancy's attacks upgrade each stage.<br>STAGE 1: Clancy fires one paintball.<br>STAGE 2: Clancy fires a second paintball each attack!<br>STAGE 3: Clancy fires 2 additional paintballs diagonally each attack!}}
Clancy shoots long-ranged bullets in different configurations at different stages, similar to [[Surge]] and [[Chester]]. At Stage 1, Clancy fires 1 bullet forward. At Stage 2, he fires 2 bullets in two waves. At Stage 3, he adds 2 additional bullets to the first wave that travel diagonally, for a total of 4 bullets in an attack.

### Super: Torrent

{{Quote|Clancy's Super upgrades each stage.<br>STAGE 1: Clancy fires a torrent of paintballs in a cone in front of him.<br>STAGE 2: Paintballs travel further.<br>STAGE 3: Paintballs deal 16% additional damage!}}
Clancy shoots 2 streams of 6 bullets each, dealing damage erratically in a cone shape. As he fires, he initially aims both streams ahead of himself, then expands them outwards to each side, and then brings them back together slightly. At Stage 2, the range of the Super's projectiles is increased, and at Stage 3, the damage of each projectile is increased by 16.7%. At all stages, the damage is variable with distance. He deals significantly less damage in the first 25% of his range, as low as half of the full damage at minimum range, but deals full damage beyond 25% range.

### Gadgets / Snappy Shooting

{{Quote|Clancy gains double tokens for 5 seconds. Cooldown: 12 seconds.}}{{Gadget|1}}
Upon activation, Clancy obtains double the amount of tokens for all paintballs he shoots for the next 5 seconds.

### Gadgets / Tactical Retreat

{{Quote|Clancy dashes and reloads 1 ammo. Cooldown: 13 seconds.}}{{Gadget|2}}
Clancy dashes forward 3.33 tiles in the direction he is facing upon using this Gadget, and also regains 1 ammo. However, the dash is cancelled if he is stunned, pulled, or knocked back. He also cannot dash through walls or water, as doing so will stop him directly on those tiles.

### Star Powers / Recon

{{Quote|Clancy starts the match with 2 tokens.}}{{StarPower|1}}
This Star Power gives Clancy 2 existent tokens at the start of the match.

### Star Powers / Pumping Up

{{Quote|Clancy reloads all of his ammo on every enemy takedown.}}{{StarPower|2}}
This Star Power allows Clancy to fully reload his ammo each time he defeats an enemy Brawler.

### Hypercharge: Recall of Duty

{{Quote|Clancy's Super paintballs pierce enemies then quickly return to him, dealing damage again.}}{{Hypercharge}}
When activated, paintballs from Clancy's Super will pierce through enemies and return to him once they have reached max range. However, if the projectiles contact a wall while coming back to him, they will despawn. The projectiles have no damage scaling when returning to Clancy. He also gains a 20% speed boost, as well as a 5% damage and shield boost.

### Tips

*Because of the large spread of Clancy's Super, it is best to move back and forth while using it. This will allow more consistent hits on the enemy
*Clancy is almost useless at the start of the match, as his low damage and mobility at Stage 1 heavily hinders his performance, so it's best to immediately aim for Stage 2 right at the start.
**At Stage 2, Clancy becomes much better thanks to the second attack projectile, which makes it easier to get to Stage 3. Around this point, Clancy should also have his Super, which can be combined with the Snappy Shooting Gadget for an almost instant Stage 3 upgrade against assassins or tanks.
**At Stage 3, Clancy is almost unstoppable, with his fast movement speed and devastating Super damage allowing him to control wide areas without dying himself.
*In [[Heist]], Clancy potentially deal a quarter of the safe's health with a single Super at Stage 3. This makes him an extremely dangerous Brawler to be left alone with the safe, but due to the spread of his Super attack, it is important to go to point-blank range to hit every shot. However, this can be dangerous for Clancy due to his rather low health pool and the usual selection of Brawlers in this gamemode being ones with high DPS, which means he could be prevent from even getting close to the safe without dying.
*If you decide to equip the Snappy Shooting Gadget, it is recommended to use it while you have full ammo for the most efficiency, as if you were to activate it while you're still reloading or have no ammo at all, its duration will be wasted. It is also a good decision to use it at the start of the game, so you can do more damage later in the game.
*Clancy cannot adjust the direction of his Super once activated, making it possible to waste a large chunk of the salvo if an enemy gets behind him. Make sure to track enemy movement during your Super and instantly readjust if you find yourself in front of the enemy.
**Alternatively, you can also use it mid-range away from your enemy, as then you will be less likely to miss and still guarantee at least 70% of your Super's maximum damage.
