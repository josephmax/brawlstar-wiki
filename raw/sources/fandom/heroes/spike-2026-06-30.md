Spike is a {{Rarity|Legendary}} [[Brawler]] with moderately low health and long range, but a very high damage output at close range, in exchange for his slow reload speed. He was one of the 15 Brawlers who released at the first Beta launch. His attack is a cactus that explodes on impact and shoots spikes in all directions. His Super lobs a thorny projectile that creates a slowing and damaging patch of spikes on the ground. His first Gadget, Popping Pincushion, allows Spike to throw a row of spikes for his next attack. His second Gadget, Life Plant, spawns a cactus that provides cover for him and his teammates, and heals them in a radius once the cactus is destroyed. His first Star Power, Fertilize, allows Spike to heal himself when his Super damages enemies. His second Star Power, Curveball, causes the spikes shot out from his attack to curve in a circular motion. His Hypercharge, Blooming Season, increases his Super's radius.

### Attack: Needle Grenade

{{Quote|Spike fires off a small cactus that explodes, shooting spikes in different directions.}}
Spike shoots a long-ranged, damaging cactus that, upon impact with a target or wall, or reaching its maximum range, explodes and sends out 6 spikes radially for 4.33 tiles. The cactus itself also deals one spike's worth of damage to enemies in a 1-tile explosion radius. The spikes' pattern is fixed, with 60 degrees between each spike, and it doesn't rotate with the angle the cactus projectile is thrown. The attack deals devastating damage at point-blank range where more spikes can hit the enemy.

### Super: Stick Around!

{{Quote|Spike lobs a thorny grenade. Enemies caught in the blast area take damage and are slowed down.}}
Spike lobs a thorny grenade over obstacles that, upon landing, creates a circular patch of spikes on the ground that lasts for 4.5 seconds, dealing up to 5 ticks of damage. Enemies within its area of effect take damage over time and are slowed by 400 points for 1.05 seconds on contact. Spike's Super will land on the ground sooner the closer he throws it to himself.

### Gadgets / Popping Pincushion

{{Quote|Spike aims and fires a wave of needles that deal 1000 damage. Cooldown: 14 seconds.}}{{Gadget|1}}
Spike shoots a row of 5 needles in the direction the Gadget is aimed. Each needle from this Gadget deals 102% the damage as one of his main attack needles. This Gadget's cooldown begins after the attack is shot.

{{Quote|Targets that are hit with 2 needles become rooted for 1 seconds.}}{{Buffie|Gadget}}
With the Buffie, the needles from this Gadget root enemies for one second if at least two of them hit, making the enemy unable to move but still able to attack. The Buffie also modifies the Gadget's damage to scale with distance. Targets near Spike's maximum range take the most damage, and targets closer to Spike take 60% of the damage.

### Gadgets / Life Plant

{{Quote|Spike grows a large cactus with 3500 health to give cover to teammates. If destroyed, the cactus bursts, and all nearby teammates recover 1680 health. Cooldown: 20 seconds.}}{{Gadget|2}}
Spike throws a projectile that spawns a large cactus that has 58.3% of his maximum health where it lands. Similar to [[Ruffs]]' Take Cover Gadget, it blocks non-piercing or bouncing/splitting narrow enemy fire. If the cactus is destroyed, the plant bursts and heals all allies in a 3.33-tile radius for 30% of Spike's maximum health. If Spike places down another cactus, the initial one will be destroyed and will heal allies. This Gadget's cooldown begins after the cactus is thrown.

{{Quote|When destroyed, the plant also deals 1200 area damage that pushes enemies back.}}{{Buffie|Gadget}}
With the Buffie, the cactus' burst also deals 1200 damage to enemies and knocks them back.

### Star Powers / Curveball

{{Quote|Spikes from the cactus grenade fly in a curving motion, making it easier to hit targets.}}{{StarPower|2}}
The spikes shot from his main attack now move in a curving clockwise motion instead of straight out, allowing Spike's attack to cover a larger effective area and ultimately hit more targets with his attack. This Star Power also effectively increases the range of the spikes.

{{Quote|Curving spikes have extended range.}}{{Buffie|Star}}

### Hypercharge: Blooming Season

{{Quote|Stick Around has 20% larger area.}}{{Hypercharge}}
When activated, Spike's Super area is increased. Despite the description, the 20% increase is of the radius and not the area. He also gains a 20% speed boost, as well as a 5% damage and shield boost.

{{Quote|Main attack needle grenades detonate twice!}}{{Buffie|Hyper}}
With the Buffie, during his Hypercharge, Spike's main attack will leave an additional cactus grenade on the ground when it initially explodes. This second grenade will detonate ~0.6 seconds after the first one, acting identically to his main attack. Increased Hypercharge Duration by 2 seconds.

### Tips / Game Modes and Maps

*Spike is versatile in [[Heist]] thanks to his high damage output: he can either go on defence with his unpredictable spread of attack and slowing Super, or go on the offense with his extreme damage output. Tanks who are favoured in this mode (i.e. [[Bull]], [[Darryl]], or [[El Primo]]) are countered by Spike, as without their Supers, they struggle to reach the opposing team's safe with their short range, and with Spike's slowing Super and damage, he can easily take them down at range. If Spike is positioned correctly or is at point-blank range of the safe, he can deal devastatingly high damage to it with his Popping Pincushion Gadget along with his high-damaging attack, which makes him a solid choice. Popping Pincushion can also serve as a fourth attack or during defence since Spike reloads slowly.
*When defending a critical objective or controlling an area, Spike's Super and his Fertilize Star Power are useful for area denial, slowing and damaging enemies while healing himself. If the enemy team is fleeing with Gems in [[Gem Grab]] or attempting to score a goal in [[Brawl Ball]], you can hamper them from doing so with your Super.

### Tips / Recommended Build

*Spike's Fertilize Star Power and Life Plant Gadget are useful for healing if your team lacks healers. If you find yourself charging your Super too often, don't hesitate to use it on yourself to heal you fully while you keep attacking enemies. If you or teammates are low on health and your Super isn't charged up, you can spawn your Life Plant Gadget down, hide behind it, and heal up. The extra healing his Life Plant provides when it's destroyed makes it even better for supporting Spike and teammates. These mechanics are also useful in [[Showdown]] when facing high-damaging enemies or in self-defense, especially in Showdown when being ambushed or pinched.
*Spike's Curveball Star Power makes his attacks generally harder to dodge for most players, but more experienced players will have less trouble evading his attack. However, Curveball also has the ability to hit enemies behind cover in ways that his normal attack cannot.
*A tip when playing against Spike: if the opposing Spike has Curveball Star Power equipped, here's what you can try in order to dodge his shots. Go a little left below the projectile (make sure not to collide with it, as it will split immediately and deal damage), just below the end of Spike's range, and as the shot splits, move upwards, go right, go down, and then go left (do this in quick succession). This means that you will spin in a clockwise circle. As Curveball always rotates at 75 degrees, his shots will split (in your vision) from right to left. Thus, walking from left to right allows you to just miss the spread of his attack. Going up and going down allows you to go back to the same position as you were before. This takes a lot of practice, but can be very effective when put into play.

### Tips / Strategies

*Spike has low health and a slow reload speed. Because of this, it's best to practice shooting around obstacles, predicting his shot pattern, and predicting the enemy's movement to prevent opportunities for the enemies to damage you. However, his high damage output compensates for his low health and allows him to defeat enemies easily.
*Spike's attack can deal devastating damage. One way to deal that maximum damage is at point-blank range of the enemy where his attack and Popping Pincushion Gadget work best at the aforementioned range. This makes him an excellent counter against close-ranged Brawlers such as [[El Primo]], [[Rosa]], [[Sam]], [[Edgar]], [[Doug]], [[Jacky]], [[Hank]], [[Kit]], [[Fang]], [[Lily]], [[Mico]], [[Kenji]], [[Bibi]], [[Buzz]], and [[Mortis]] who have to get close to Spike to damage him.
*Spike is capable of dealing extremely high damage to grouped-up enemies due to the multiple spikes shot out from his attack on impact. His Super's area damage can also spell trouble for grouped-up enemies since they can't easily escape from Spike's attack or Popping Pincushion Gadget owing to the slow effect. This becomes more effective using his Sticky Spikes Mythic gear.
*The 6 spikes that fly out from his attack always spread out in the same pattern (60-degree angles, or 75 degrees with Curveball) no matter what your position or rotation is. It is stable for you to practice this to know where the spikes will land, making it easier for you to hit enemies around corners and obstacles or to check bushes. This regularity comes in handy, imposing your attacks near a target is vitally impactful as it increases the chance of multiple spikes hitting the same target.
*Use Life Plant Gadget to redirect shots such as auto-aimed shots, [[Meeple]]'s main attack, or [[Pierce]]'s attacks created by picking up his shells.
