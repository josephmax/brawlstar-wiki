Janet is a {{Rarity|Mythic}} [[Brawler]] who could be unlocked as a [[Brawl Pass]] reward at Tier 30 in Season 12: The Stuntshow. She has moderate health and damage output, a variable attack range, and immense utility with her Super. She was the 56th Brawler added to the game. Her attack is a shockwave in a cone that narrows and lengthens the longer it is aimed. Janet's Super allows her to continuously fly over obstacles while dropping bombs onto enemies that deal very low area damage. Janet's first Gadget, Drop the Bass, deploys a loudspeaker that deals damage to enemies in a large radius. Her second Gadget, Backstage Pass, affects Janet's next attack to make her jump backwards. Her first Star Power, Stage View, allows Janet to see enemies in a large area of bushes while her Super is active. Her second Star Power, Vocal Warm Up, reduces the time of Janet's attack focusing from minimum range to maximum. Her Hypercharge, Magnum Opus, allows her Super to be controlled more easily during its duration.

### Attack: High Note

{{Quote|Janet sings a loud note. The longer she holds the note, the more focus it will become and the farther it will fly.}}
Janet's attack is a piercing shockwave that deals moderate damage. When initially aiming or using autofire, the attack is wide and has a short range, but the longer the attack is aimed, the less wide and the more range the attack gains, which stops upon reaching its maximum distance. If Janet has no ammo, aiming the attack will not change its shape.

### Super: Crescendo

{{Quote|Janet uses her jetpack to take to the skies for a moment, where she attacks opponents from above for the full duration.}}Janet flies up into the sky for 6 seconds, boosting her movement speed and dropping bombs every 0.76 seconds onto enemies under her. The bombs deal area damage in a 2-tile radius. Like [[Nani]]'s Super, Janet will fly in a straight line if she is not controlled. She cannot reload or heal while her Super is active. While in the air, Janet is completely immune to all damage besides status effects and damage applied over time which were applied to her prior to using her Super. When using her Super, she cannot occupy the zone in [[Hot Zone]], she can't pick up Power Cubes in [[Showdown]], and she can't teleport with teleporters. If her final destination is an obstacle or in the middle of a lake, she will automatically be pushed to the closest ground.

### Gadgets / Drop The Bass

{{Quote|Janet deploys a speaker that deals 200 damage per second to all enemies in the area until it gets destroyed. Cooldown: 18 seconds.}}{{Gadget|1}}Upon activation, Janet drops a loudspeaker that deals 10% of Janet's main attack damage per second to enemies in its 8-tile radius. The turret has 1000 health, will be destroyed if Janet places another loudspeaker, and decays by 100 health until it's destroyed, lasting for up to 10 seconds if it doesn't take any damage. Janet cannot activate this Gadget while her Super is active.

### Gadgets / Backstage Pass

{{Quote|Janet's next High Note pushes her back, even over walls. The more focused her attack is, the further she'll be pushed back. Cooldown: 16 seconds.}}{{Gadget|2}}Activating this Gadget allows Janet's next attack to make her jump backwards into the air. The longer the attack is aimed, the farther Janet travels and the longer Janet stays in the air, where she is completely immune to all damage besides status effects and damage applied over time. She can travel up to 6 tiles normally. If required to cross over a larger amount of blocks or over lakes, the distance will be increased up to a limit or to the nearest piece of ground. A Gadget symbol will glow above Janet's head signaling this Gadget's usage, as well as a glowing attack joystick. This Gadget's cooldown starts after the attack is shot.

### Star Powers / Stage View

{{Quote|When in the air with her jetpack, Janet reveals a large area of bushes around herself.}}{{StarPower|1}}When Janet is using her Super, she and all allies can see all enemies in bushes 10 tiles farther than usual from Janet.

### Star Powers / Vocal Warm Up

{{Quote|Janet's attack focuses 30% faster.}}{{StarPower|2}}This Star Power causes Janet's attack to focus from minimum range to maximum range at a faster speed.

### Hypercharge: Magnum Opus

{{Quote|Janet retains full movement control during her Super.}}{{Hypercharge}}
When activated, for the duration of Janet's Super, she has complete control of her movement as if she was on the ground, and she also no longer automatically flies forwards during it. In addition, her Super is affected by the Hypercharge movement speed boost, and it stacks with other speed boosts, such as [[Max]]'s Super or [[Stu]]'s Speed Zone Gadget. Janet also gains a 20% speed boost, as well as a 5% damage and shield boost.

### Tips / Game Modes and Maps

*She is very strong in [[Gem Grab]], able to hold off gems in the countdown. It is advisable to play her as the gem carrier, and play defensively as gem collector, or offensively. She can carry all 10 gems and in the 15 second countdown she can flee and use her Super so that enemies can no longer attack her.
*If Janet is the Big Brawler in [[Big Game]], she cannot be attacked when she uses her Super. Her Backstage Pass Gadget can let her escape the Hunters more effectively and her Drop the Bass Gadget lets her prepare against the Hunters' approach. Thus, the Hunters struggle in taking down the Big Brawler.

### Tips / Recommended Build

*Janet's Drop The Bass Gadget is quite useful in maps covered in bushes, like [[Snake Prairie]] and [[Sapphire Plains]], as enemies in her Gadget's radius receive damage, causing them to be revealed, much like [[Grom]]'s Watchtower Gadget, [[Bo]]'s Circling Eagle Star Power, [[Rosa]]'s Unfriendly Bushes Gadget, and [[Tara]]'s Psychic Enhancer Gadget.
*Her Drop The Bass Gadget can prevent enemies from healing, making this a very effective strategy to prevent low-health enemies from being able to recover and fight back. It has a low health, so make sure you put it behind a wall or a place where enemies can't hit it easily.
*Keep in mind that Janet's Super does not drop a bomb directly below her, but rather firing them a short distance forward. This means that circling a stationary enemy will likely only hit them once.
*The Backstage Pass Gadget is sometimes useful in close-range situations against quick unloading enemies since it may cause the enemy to miss all three shots when you suddenly jump when attacking, reducing the chance of getting defeated and making it easy to finish off the enemy.

### Tips / Other

*Janet's attack special main attack ability is good for the right situation like if you need to hit your enemy from far away it's obviously better to charge it. This is not what you have to do on melee situations, because you lose time trying to get more range and only one projectile can hit the same enemy.
