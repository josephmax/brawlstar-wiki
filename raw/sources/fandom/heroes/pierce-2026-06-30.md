Pierce is a {{Rarity|Legendary}} [[Brawler]] who has moderately low health, a very long range, and a variable damage output, but suffers by having an extremely slow reload speed. He was the 98th Brawler added to the game. He attacks by firing water towards enemies that drop shells on the ground upon hitting a target. Upon picking the shells up, he automatically shoots towards the closest target, which passes through obstacles. For his Super, Pierce marks all targets in the radius after a delay, and then automatically attacks by shooting projectiles that home in on the enemies. His first Gadget, Bottomless Mags, makes him drop a shell on the ground, while reloading one ammo slot. His second Gadget, You Only Brawl Twice, provides a shield that absorbs shells and decays over time. His first Star Power, Mission: Swimpossible, slows enemies down if they are hit by Pierce's last ammo attack. His second Star Power, Slip 'n Snipe, grants Pierce a brief movement speed boost upon picking a shell up. His Hypercharge, Second Wave, adds a second wave of Super projectile that deals less damage than the first wave, and allows his Super projectiles to pass through obstacles and pierce through enemies.

### Attack: Pool Sniper

{{Quote|Pierce fires a powerful long range shot. If the projectile hits an enemy, Pierce drops a shell next to himself. Collecting a shell fires a piercing shot automatically towards the nearest visible target. Pierce can only reload by collecting shells or depleting ammo. The last ammo in a clip deals extra damage.}}Pierce fires a water projectile at long range that deals low damage and drops a shell if it hits an enemy. When he only has one ammo, Pierce's ammo bar will turn yellow, and the attack will deal 58% more damage. Unlike other Brawlers, Pierce cannot automatically reload; instead, he must pick up shells, which will restore one ammo to him. Picking up a shell will also shoot a water projectile at the closest enemy, dealing low damage and piercing the environment but not enemies. These shells last for 8 seconds each, and when not picked up, they will despawn. If multiple players on a team are playing as Pierce, each Pierce can only pick up their own shells, and will ignore ally Pierce's shells. He can also reload if his ammo bar is empty, which will begin to fill a bar shown under his health over 3 seconds, automatically reloading all three ammo once it fills. Unlike most Brawlers that respawn with one ammo, Pierce respawns with all three.

### Super: Fish in a Barrel

{{Quote|Pierce marks an area, then fires at any Brawlers hit with a piercing shot. Each shot that hits will also drop a shell next to Pierce.}}Pierce marks the area in a 3-tile radius, and, after a 1-second delay, he fires homing attacks towards any enemies caught in the radius. These projectiles deal high damage, and pass through obstacles but not enemies. Similar to his attack, the Super drops shells on hit that similarly last for 8 seconds before they despawn.

### Gadgets / You Only Brawl Twice

{{Quote|Pierce will absorb all shells and gain 1200 shield for each one, while also pushing back nearby enemies. Cooldown: 13 seconds.}}{{Gadget|2}}[[File:Hardcore Icon.png|left|40x40px|link=]]When activated, Pierce absorbs all shells that are on the ground and grants himself a consumable shield that blocks a large amount of damage for each shell and knocks nearby enemies back by 2 tiles. The shield decays for 5% health every 0.25 seconds, similar to other decaying shield abilities. If an attack deals more damage than the shield's current health, the shield disappears, and Pierce with the shield is damaged by the remaining damage. [[Colette]]'s attack and Super will deal damage based on the Brawler's max health when this shield is active. Enemies damaging those with the shield on can still charge their Supers. If there are no shells on the map, Pierce can still knock enemies back within range, but no shield is created.

### Tips

* Pierce's unique mechanics reward consistency in hitting your shots. As such, play conservatively until you get your Super, which will allow you to get lots of shells that allow for a much more aggressive playstyle.
** Alternatively, you can play a much more aggressive and close-ranged role to secure shots more easily, though keep in mind that despite his relatively high DPS, Pierce lacks the health to deal with typical aggressive Brawlers.
*Collect the shells (or use Bottomless Mags Gadget) just after you use the last ammo so the shell gives you the yellow ammo that deals a lot more damage, which increases their effectiveness.
*Pierce's fairly low damage when compared to other Marksmen and his high-risk-high-reward playstyle mean that he tends to be outmatched by other snipers in long-ranged maps. As such, play him in medium-ranged maps, where he's able to bully shorter-ranged brawlers that appear there more often.
*Sometimes, it's more effective to keep only one ammo in Pierce's clip so that he can deal bonus damage. This is especially so when he's using his Mission: Swimpossible Star Power, which is one of his only ways to defend himself against aggressive Brawlers.
*If you're fighting aggressive Brawlers, keep a cache of shells near you at all times: when approached, walk into the cache to overwhelm your attacker with shots. Keep in mind that the shells have to be renewed frequently since they despawn after some amount of time.
*Pay attention to where you're standing: Pierce's shells may sometimes drop behind walls - the direction the shells drop is random. It might make it a hassle for him to pick them up and often cost him precious time.
*When fighting against Pierce with a Brawler that possesses area control abilities, lay hazards near any shells near him: as Pierce is very reliant on these shells, most players will often risk picking up the shells. For Pierce players, be aware of your surroundings and avoid recklessly picking up shells especially on low health, since Pierce doesn't have much to begin with.
*Ironically, Pierce doesn't really have any proper piercing attacks in his kit, as his shell projectiles disappear upon hitting their chosen target. As such, make it your priority to target spawner Brawlers as their pets can make you waste ammo and prevent you from loading shells, as well as shield their owners by making the attacks target them.
*Pierce's shots drop shells when hitting base bots in brawl arena, allowing him to clear them very efficiently.
